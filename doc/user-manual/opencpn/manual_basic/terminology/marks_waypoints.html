Marks & Waypoints

Marks are used to mark points of interest (POI), See Use Marks.
Routes are a sequence of Waypoints for navigation purposes. See Use Routes.
If a Mark is used in a Route, then it is converted to a Waypoint and added to the Route with a "mark flag" to show that this particular point was once a Mark.
If a Route is deleted, then all Waypoints that have a "mark flag" are reconverted back to Marks and the other points are deleted.

Technical: Routes, Waypoints, Marks, GPX and GUID

Marks, Routes and Waypoints

Please refer to Use GPX Files. In GPX Files, (for better or worse, it’s not going to change):
* Marks have a record label of <wpt>
* Waypoints (always in a Route) have a record label of <rtept>
* When a Mark becomes part of a Route its record label changes from <wpt> to <rtept> and two "mark flag" fields are added (sse examples below):

  * %%<opencpn:viz_name>1</opencpn:viz_name>%% <-- Additional Field
  * %%<opencpn:shared>1</opencpn:shared>%%     <-- Additional Field
* When a //Route// is deleted //Waypoints// with a "mark flag" are converted back to **%%<wpt>%%** and the two additional fields are dropped.
* A Mark may also be included in zero to many routes.
* A //Waypoint// cannot exist without being in a //Route// (it must be included in 1 to many existing routes and unlike a //Mark//, it is also deleted when the last route it is a part of is deleted).
* It is not possible to have a //Mark// in a layer and then import that //Mark// from a GPX file.
* Logic used for preventing duplicate //Mark// and //Waypoint// entities during import evaluates the "Name" and position ("lat" + "lon"), because the entity may not have the OpenCPN-specific GUID extension.
* Import of a Route GPX File with duplicate waypoints will result in a message, and the existing waypoints will be shared (no duplicates are imported).

GUID

  • The "GUID" field represents the internal, globally unique identifier of a navobject in OpenCPN.

  • All navobjects with the exception of track points ("trkpt" entity in the GPX file) have a "GUID" produced by OpenCPN.

  • Two objects with the same "GUID" cannot exist at the same time.

Test-Route

Test-Route deleted leaving Marks.

Blue Ball gpx record as an independent Mark (not part of a route)

<wpt lat="41.492943333" lon="-71.350785000">   <<-- Note: wpt
  <time>2024-05-31T22:46:32Z</time>
  <name>Blue Ball</name>
  <sym>Symbol-Circle-Blue</sym>
  <type>WPT</type>                               <<-- Note: WPT
  <extensions>
    <opencpn:guid>22850000-5180-44ab-b145-ef1f4e3effff</opencpn:guid>
    <opencpn:viz_name>1</opencpn:viz_name>
    <opencpn:arrival_radius>0.050</opencpn:arrival_radius>
    <opencpn:waypoint_range_rings visible="false" number="0" step="1" units="0" colour="#FF0000" />
    <opencpn:scale_min_max UseScale="true" ScaleMin="2147483646" ScaleMax="0" />
  </extensions>
</wpt>
